Safety

If you're in crisis or thinking about hurting yourself: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) any time — it's free and trained people answer, or chat at 988lifeline.org. If you might be in immediate danger, call 911. (U.S.)

This is not a crisis service

The Map is not therapy, counseling, or an emergency service, and it does not provide medical or mental-health advice.

What happens if something sounds unsafe

If the conversation suggests a teen may be in danger, being hurt or exploited, or seriously unsafe, the Map pauses and shows the resources above. In some cases a designated OTS responder may be alerted so a person is aware and can help connect the teen to real support. That alert contains no quotes and no transcript — only that a safety event occurred, its type, an event ID, and the teen's first name and age. Safety details are never included in the report to the parent, OTS will not automatically contact a parent in every case, and abuse- or exploitation-related concerns are never routed to the parent.

Your teen's privacy

The teen sees their result first and chooses what, if anything, the parent receives. See Privacy.
